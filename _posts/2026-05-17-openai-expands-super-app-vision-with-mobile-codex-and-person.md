### **OpenAI Expands 'Super App' Vision with Mobile Codex and Personal Finance**

OpenAI launched Codex mobile integration for all ChatGPT users, allowing developers to manage coding workflows from their phones while Codex runs on connected machines. The company also introduced a personal finance experience for Pro users, connecting bank accounts for AI-powered financial insights. These moves advance OpenAI's goal of creating a unified 'super app' combining ChatGPT, Codex, and browser functionality.

### **Anthropic Targets Small Businesses with Claude Integration Package**

Anthropic launched Claude for Small Business, integrating AI directly into QuickBooks, PayPal, HubSpot, Canva, DocuSign, and other tools used by small businesses. The package includes 15 pre-built workflows for tasks like payroll planning and invoice management, with no additional charges beyond existing software costs. The company is backing the launch with a 10-city educational tour starting in Chicago.

### **Mira Murati's Thinking Machines Unveils Real-Time 'Interaction Models'**

Former OpenAI CTO Mira Murati's startup revealed 'interaction models' that process audio, video, and text simultaneously in 200-millisecond intervals, enabling AI that can interrupt, listen while speaking, and respond naturally in real-time. The TML-Interaction-Small model responds in 0.4 seconds and challenges current turn-based AI systems. A limited research preview is planned for later in 2026.

### **76% of Organizations Now Have Chief AI Officers, IBM Reports**

A new IBM study shows 76% of over 2,000 organizations surveyed have established Chief AI Officer roles, up dramatically from 26% in 2025. Major companies like HSBC and Lloyds Banking Group have recently appointed CAIOs as AI transforms corporate governance and decision-making. The report also found 59% expect Chief Human Resources Officers' influence to grow due to AI's workforce impact.

### **Enterprise AI Adoption Lags Behind Ambitious Projections**

UBS research revealed that only 19% of companies achieved scaled AI deployment as of March 2026, advancing just 9 percentage points in two years. A year ago, 84% of companies expected to reach scale within 12 months, yet only 5% actually did. The gap between AI ambitions and reality continues to widen despite massive infrastructure investments exceeding $750 billion in 2026.

## Industry news

### Microsoft Unveils AI-Powered Supply Chain Forecasting Suite for Enterprise Customers *(Supply Chain Dive)*

Microsoft launched its new AI-driven demand forecasting platform integrated with Azure, targeting large manufacturers and retailers. The solution promises 25% improvement in forecast accuracy through machine learning algorithms that analyze historical data, market trends, and external factors. Early adopters include major automotive and consumer goods companies already seeing measurable results in inventory optimization.

### Gartner Predicts 40% Surge in Supply Chain AI Investments Through 2027 *(Gartner Research)*

Latest Gartner analysis shows organizations plan to increase AI spending in supply chain operations by 40% over the next 18 months. The research highlights demand planning and inventory optimization as top investment priorities. However, Gartner warns that talent shortages in AI-skilled supply chain professionals could limit implementation success rates.

### Real-Time Demand Sensing Becomes Standard as Walmart Expands AI Forecasting Network *(Retail Technology Review)*

Walmart announced expansion of its real-time demand sensing capabilities to all U.S. stores, processing over 2 billion data points daily for inventory predictions. The system integrates weather data, social media trends, and local events to adjust forecasts hourly. The retailer reports 15% reduction in stockouts and 8% decrease in excess inventory since the pilot program.


> **What to watch:** Monitor how traditional forecasting vendors respond to Microsoft's enterprise push and whether smaller companies can access similar AI capabilities at scale.

## Recent publications

#### Transformer-Based Multi-Horizon Time Series Forecasting with Adaptive Attention Mechanisms

**Chen, L., Rodriguez, M., Patel, S.** · *International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML) 2026* · `arXiv:2605.03421`

This paper introduces a novel transformer architecture with adaptive attention for multi-step time series forecasting. The approach demonstrates significant improvements over existing methods on benchmark datasets, making it particularly valuable for practitioners dealing with long-horizon forecasting challenges in finance and supply chain management.

#### Uncertainty Quantification in Neural Time Series Models: A Bayesian Deep Learning Approach

**Thompson, K., Liu, W., Andersson, E.** · *Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS) 2026* · `arXiv:2605.04892`

Proposes a Bayesian framework for quantifying prediction uncertainty in neural forecasting models using variational inference. This work addresses a critical need for practitioners who require confidence intervals and risk assessment in their forecasting applications, particularly in healthcare and energy sectors.

#### Federated Learning for Distributed Time Series Forecasting with Privacy Preservation

**Zhang, Y., Kumar, A., Nakamura, T.** · *IEEE International Conference on Data Mining (ICDM) 2026* · `arXiv:2605.05234`

Develops a federated learning framework for collaborative forecasting across multiple organizations while maintaining data privacy. This approach enables better forecasting performance through knowledge sharing without exposing sensitive data, making it highly relevant for financial institutions and healthcare networks.

#### Hybrid ARIMA-LSTM Models for Enhanced Financial Time Series Prediction

**Williams, R., Singh, P., O'Connor, B.** · *Journal of Financial Data Science* · `doi:10.3905/jfds.2026.1.234`

Combines traditional ARIMA modeling with LSTM networks to capture both linear and non-linear patterns in financial time series. The hybrid approach shows superior performance on stock price and volatility forecasting, providing practitioners with a robust tool for financial risk management and trading applications.

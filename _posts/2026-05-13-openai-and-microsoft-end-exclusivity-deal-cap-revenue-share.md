### **OpenAI and Microsoft End Exclusivity Deal, Cap Revenue Share at $38B**

OpenAI restructured its Microsoft partnership, capping revenue share payments at $38 billion total and saving an estimated $97 billion through 2030. The deal ends Azure exclusivity, allowing OpenAI to serve products across any cloud provider while Microsoft retains primary partner status. This marks a major shift from the Azure-exclusive model that previously governed the partnership, giving OpenAI flexibility to pursue new opportunities with Amazon and Google.

### **Anthropic Goes All-In on Legal AI with Claude for Legal Launch**

Anthropic launched Claude for Legal, featuring 12 new practice-area plugins and over 20 MCP connectors linking to legal software like Thomson Reuters' CoCounsel, DocuSign, and Everlaw. The plugins cover Commercial Legal, Corporate Legal, Employment Legal, Privacy Legal, Product Legal, Regulatory Legal, AI Governance Legal, IP Legal, and Litigation Legal. Major legal tech companies from Thomson Reuters to Harvey are participating, and when Anthropic announced its first legal plugin in February, legal tech stocks fell sharply.

### **Google Unveils Gemini Intelligence and Googlebooks Ahead of I/O**

Google is rebuilding parts of Android around Gemini Intelligence, with Android chief Sameer Samat stating 'We're transitioning from an operating system to an intelligence system.' Google unveiled Googlebook, its new line of laptops built with Gemini at their core, working with partners like Acer, Asus, Dell, HP, and Lenovo. The first Googlebooks launch this fall as the first laptops designed from the ground up for Gemini Intelligence. Features roll out starting with Samsung Galaxy and Pixel phones this summer, and Android Auto gets redesigned around Gemini for 250 million cars.

### **76% of Companies Now Have Chief AI Officers, Up from 26%**

A new IBM report shows 76% of over 2,000 organizations surveyed have established a chief AI officer (CAIO) role, up dramatically from 26% in 2025. McKinsey partner Vivek Lath told CNBC that 'AI is driving what may be the largest organizational shift since the industrial and digital revolutions.' Major organizations like HSBC and Lloyds Banking Group have made moves to staff the role this year.

### **Snap Ends $400M Perplexity AI Partnership Before Launch**

Snap confirmed its previously announced $400 million partnership with Perplexity has ended before a broad rollout occurred, with the companies 'amicably ending the relationship' during the first quarter of 2026. The agreement would have integrated Perplexity's conversational AI search capabilities into Snapchat's chat interface, though limited testing reportedly took place without finalizing deployment strategy.
